abstract: "This study investigates the effect of the major sudden stratospheric warming (SSW) event of 2009 on small-scale gravity wave (GW) activity in the ionosphere. Small-scale fluctuations with periods of 10–90 min from GPS TEC data were used as a proxy for GW activity. TEC data from five stations around 60°N were analyzed. During the initial phase of the SSW, ionospheric GW activity increased, but during the peak phase and afterward, conditions were less favorable for upward GW propagation, leading to reduced GW activity. Similar reductions were observed in the middle atmosphere (65–100 km) from SABER temperature profiles. Reductions were more prominent during daytime and varied longitudinally."
