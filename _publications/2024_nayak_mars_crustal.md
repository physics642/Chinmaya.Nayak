abstract: >
  This study uses Mars Atmosphere and Volatile EvolutioN (MAVEN) observations of
  electron density and magnetic field for a period of four Martian years (MYs 33–36)
  (~8 Earth years) to investigate the effects of Martian crustal magnetic fields on
  the distribution and variability of Mars’ ionosphere. The results show a clear
  enhancement in electron density in the southern hemisphere in regions of strong
  crustal magnetic fields between longitudes 120° and 240°, consistent with earlier
  studies. In contrast, the corresponding northern hemisphere region exhibits reduced
  electron density relative to adjacent longitude sectors. These effects are primarily
  dayside phenomena. On the nightside, electron density in the central longitude
  region is slightly reduced below 200 km, while above this altitude the effects are
  less pronounced. A significant hemispheric asymmetry is observed during daytime
  across all seasons in regions of enhanced crustal fields, whereas nightside
  asymmetry remains relatively uniform across longitudes, indicating a weakened
  influence of crustal fields. Nevertheless, the southern hemisphere maintains a
  stronger nightside ionosphere during all seasons except summer.
