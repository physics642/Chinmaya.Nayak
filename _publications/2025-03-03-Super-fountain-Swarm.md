## Journal Articles

### 1. Nayak, Chinmaya; Buchert, Stephan; Yiğit, Erdal; Ankita, M.; Singh, Satyavir; Tulasi Ram, S.; Dimri, A. P. (2025)  
**Title:** "Topside Low-Latitude Ionospheric Response to the 10–11 May 2024 Super Geomagnetic Storm as Observed by Swarm: The Strongest Storm-Time Super-Fountain During the Swarm Era?"  
**Journal:** Journal of Geophysical Research: Space Physics, Volume 130, Issue 3, e2024JA033340  
**DOI:** [10.1029/2024JA033340](https://doi.org/10.1029/2024JA033340)  
**URL:** [Publisher Link](https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1029/2024JA033340)  
**PDF:** [Download](https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/pdf/10.1029/2024JA033340)  

**Keywords:** May 2024 storm, geomagnetic storm, super-fountain, equatorial ionization anomaly, equatorial ionosphere, prompt penetration electric field  

**Abstract:**  
The current study explores how the topside equatorial and low-latitude ionosphere responded to the super geomagnetic storm on 10–11 May 2024, using in situ data from the Swarm constellation. During the storm's main phase, enhancements in the EIA (equatorial ionization anomaly) were observed, forming strong super-fountains. The EIAs extended toward latitudes beyond ±30° of the magnetic equator, with the crests shifted to ~25° of the magnetic equator in both hemispheres during the storm's main phase. Swarm-A observed the strongest storm-time super-fountain during its entire age (2013–present) with nearly 500% increase in the crest density compared to quiet-time conditions. During the recovery phase, the EIA was entirely suppressed, with density crests over the magnetic equator and troughs around ~10°–15° magnetic dip latitude.
